SIGBPS Track: Business Processes in Healthcare Service

Track Chairs:

Nanda Kumar (City University of New York)

Track Description:

Over the last several decades, computer-based information systems have been used to transform the way white-collar work is done. Specifically, the emergence of cheap, networked personal computers has allowed organizations to standardize many aspects of white-collar work across space and time by embedding and enforcing organizational routines via workflow software. However, research on the impact of information technology in the healthcare industry - one of the most information intensive industries has produced mixed results (Walker 2005; Diamond and Shirky 2008). We invite interested researchers to submit their work on topics outlined below (as well as on other relevant themes that explore the role of IT in healthcare).

> Standardization Practices and Workflow Management
> Adoption and Diffusion of IS in healthcare
> HCI in Healthcare
> Role of Gamification
> Privacy and Security Issues
> Impact of IT on Patient Safety and other relevant outcomes such as efficiency
> Digital Divide; Access Issues
> Role of Mobile Computing
> Big Data and Healthcare
